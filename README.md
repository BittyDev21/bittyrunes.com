### 🟠 What is Bitty?

A character born from **Here Comes Bitcoin** — an open-source educational initiative by **Spiral** (Block, Inc.). On **May 8, 2024**, Spiral declared all assets free for anyone to use, anywhere, without permission. No copyright. Forever.

> *"Bitcoin has rules. Promoting bitcoin should not."* — [@spiralbtc](https://x.com/spiralbtc/status/1788239963626123600)

On **May 1, 2025**, Bitty's animations were merged into **bitcoin.org** via [PR #4254](https://github.com/bitcoin-dot-org/Bitcoin.org/pull/4254) by Cobra-Bitcoin.

On **February 4, 2026**, at block **935,003**, Bitty was etched onto the Bitcoin blockchain as **Rune #209,730** — forever.

### 📖 The Record — Three Layers of History

**Layer I · The Foundation** — In 2008, Satoshi published the [Bitcoin white paper](https://bitcoin.org/bitcoin.pdf). In 2009, the Genesis Block was mined. In April 2024, the Runes protocol launched at halving block 840,000 — bringing native fungible tokens to Bitcoin L1. No sidechains. No bridges. Pure Bitcoin.

**Layer II · The Character** — Spiral created Here Comes Bitcoin, making Bitcoin funnier, friendlier, and more accessible. On May 1, 2025, Bitty was merged into bitcoin.org. A hidden easter egg. A grumpy orange mascot carrying the ₿ on his chest.

**Layer III · The Act** — On February 4, 2026, at block 935,003, BITTY•THE•BITCOIN•MASCOT was etched via the Runes protocol. The inscription landed on a sat mined on May 24, 2015 — Epoch 1. Five days later, 21,000 BITTY were sent to Satoshi's Genesis Wallet for $0.19 USD. Permanently. Verifiably. Irreversibly.
